The Housing Crisis

Traditional
homebuilding
is broken.

Over 200 years of stick-built construction hasn't kept pace with the needs of modern families. Rising costs, labor shortages, and inefficient processes have made quality housing unattainable for millions.

"Modular construction wastes 5% of materials. Traditional methods waste 20%."
01
Outdated methods
Stick-built construction relies on sequential trade labor — each trade waits on the last, compounding delays and cost overruns. 200+ yrs without fundamental change.
02
Spiraling costs
Unpredictable material pricing and on-site waste make budgets unreliable. Weather delays add months and tens of thousands to every project. 20% waste vs. 5% in controlled fabrication.
03
Labor shortages
The skilled trades workforce is shrinking. Development pipelines stall as contractors compete for the same dwindling pool of workers, slowing housing supply when demand is highest.
